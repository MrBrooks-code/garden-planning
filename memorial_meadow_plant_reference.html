How to Read These Cards

Reading a card

Tier badge (left edge color & circle): how essential to the design. Tier 1 keystone plants the design depends on. Tier 2 important and substitutable. Tier 3 nice-to-have, easy to add later.

Bloom bar: months of flowering, shaded in the species’ bloom color. Foliage plants (grasses, sedges, ginger) are shaded in their seasonal foliage color rather than flowers.

Sun: Full = 6+ hours direct. Part = 3–6 hours / dappled. Shade = under 3 hours direct.

Moisture: this site is clay-heavy but not wet outside the south-bulge pocket. Plants marked ♢ (wet-loving) concentrate in the wet pocket where the gutter empties. Dry/Average plants tolerate the rest of the meadow with seasonal watering during establishment.

Spacing: plant centers, not pot-edges. Plant tighter than nursery tags suggest — clay plus sheet-mulch closes canopy faster than expected.

Qty: the count purchased for this design. Three palm sedges below the brief’s six are intentional — the site is too dry for six.

Placement: where this species sits in the L-band design (segment 1–5 or around-hazelnut).

Pollinator note: the ecological reason this species is here. Specialist bees, host plants, and migration-fuel relationships are called out where they exist.

Catalog Cross-Reference

All 31 species are confirmed available at the sale catalog (plants1.png and plants2.png). When standing at the tables on sale day, the Latin names are alphabetical — cross-reference with the cards above. If a species you want is sold out, the cards note which neighbors are good substitutes (similar bloom time, same role, same conditions).

Catalog plants not in this design but worth considering as substitutes (visible in plants2.png):

  • Symphyotrichum sericeum — Silky Aster, alternate fall aster if Smooth Blue is sold out
  • Verbena hastata — Blue Vervain, wet-site tolerant (alternate for the wet pocket)
  • Carex pensylvanica — Pennsylvania Sedge, drought-tolerant alternative to Palm Sedge for the rest of the band
  • Carex bicknellii — Bicknell’s Sedge, sun-tolerant prairie sedge, also drier-tolerant than Palm Sedge
  • Elymus hystrix — Bottlebrush Grass, shade-tolerant grass for the around-hazelnut zone
  • Thalictrum dasycarpum — Purple Meadow Rue, vertical interest, part-shade tolerant
