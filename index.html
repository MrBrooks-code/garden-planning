Memorial Meadow

A native-plant memorial for recently-lost dogs

Racine · Wisconsin · Zone 6a

One hundred twenty-five native plants across thirty-one species, arranged along an L-shaped meadow band in southeastern Wisconsin. The memorial plant is Wild Columbine — chosen because it self-seeds and returns in slightly different places each year, like memory.

The Design at a Glance

The garden wraps from the southern gate to the northern peach trees in an L-shape, following a formal-to-wild gradient along the path arc. Six functional segments:

  • 1. South Bulge — the wet pocket, where the gutter empties. Two existing white turtleheads anchor the moisture-loving plants. Most composed end of the meadow.
  • 2. Elbow Corner — the path-turn, where Mountain Mint forces a scent-pivot at the user’s body line.
  • 3. Lower West Arm — structural anchors (Joe Pye, Culver’s Root, Ironweed) merging visually with the existing redbud and hazelnut.
  • 4. Middle West Arm — scattered matrix, no drifts. The body of the meadow; the eye rests on weave rather than composition.
  • 5. Upper West Arm — sparse taper. The meadow doesn’t end — it fades into lawn.
  • Around-Hazelnut — the memorial heart. Wild Columbines on the north side of the hazelnut bushes meet the return walk at eye level.

About

This site documents a memorial garden in Racine, Wisconsin. The plant list was finalized from a local native plant sale; the geometry was agreed via a hand-drawn map; the spatial placement and printable guides were designed in Claude Code.

Project notes, the source-of-truth ASCII site map, and the design brief are in project.md at the repository root.
